Types of trails

Long-distance footpaths (GR)
Signposted in white and red. They exceed 50 kilometres in length or days of route.
Short-distance footpaths (PR)
These are marked with white and yellow signs and are usually between 10 and 50 kilometres long, although in some cases they may be shorter.
Local Footpaths (SL)
These are white and green in colour and are usually limited to a specific locality. Their distance may vary, but as a general rule they do not exceed 10 kilometres in length and are of little or no difficulty.
Livestock trails
These are signposted with metal posts in the ground or the typical sign showing the figure of cattle and a zigzag path in yellow and orange.
Equine Itineraries (IE)
These are marked in orange and tend to share the route with cattle trails and the rest of the footpaths.
